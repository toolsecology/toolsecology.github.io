What's Body Mass Index (BMI)?

Body mass index (BMI) was born in the 19th century, when a Belgian statistician named Adolphe Quetelet, a pioneer in studies of human growth, developed the Quetelet index of obesity in 1832. He observed that people's weight increased as the square of the height. 

BMI is a quick and easy method to classify people according to their weight: underweight, normal weight, overweight and obesity and to estimate the risk of diseases. 

It also allows people to compare their own weight with that of the general population. 

BMI does not directly measure the percentage of body fat. It is only moderately correlated with more direct measurements. So even if two people have the same BMI, their body fat levels may be different.

How is BMI calculated? 

BMI is calculated the same way for everyone. The formula depends on the units system used:

Metric system

  BMI is calculated by taking a person's weight and dividing it by the square of his height in meters. The result is expressed in kilograms / meter2. 
  
BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]2     

Example: Suppose you are 1.75m tall and weigh 70kg. 

- We apply the BMI formula directly
BMI = 70/(1.75)2 = 22.9 kg/m2    

US System

  BMI is calculated by taking a person's weight in pounds and dividing it by the square of his height in inches and then multiplying by 704. The result is expressed in kilograms / meter2. 

height (in) = (feet X 12) + inches 
BMI = weight (lb) / [height (in)]2 X 704      

Example: Suppose you are 5'8"and weigh 140 pounds. 
- We convert the height to inches. 
5'8"= (5 X 12) + 8 = 68 inches 
- Then use the BMI formula. 
BMI = 140 / (68)2 X 704 = 21.3 kg / m2  

UK system

  BMI is calculated as for the US system. But you also need to convert the weight into pounds. 
  
height (in) = (feet X 12) + inches weight (lb) = (stones X 14) + pounds 
BMI = weight (lb) / [height (in)]2 X 704      

Example: Suppose you are 5'6"and weigh 9 stones and 10 pounds. 
- We convert the height to inches. 5'6"= (5 X 12) + 6 = 66 inches 
- We convert the weight to pounds. 9 stones and 10 pounds = (9 X 14) + 10 = 136 pounds 
- Then use the BMI formula. 
BMI = 136 / (66)2 X 704 = 22.0 kg / m2

What are the BMI categories?

BMI is interpreted differently for children and adults, even though it is calculated using the same formula. The BMI of children and teenagers is specific to age and gender, as the amount of body fat changes with age and varies between girls and boys.

For children and teenagers: 

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) recommends using population percentiles as a means of classification:  

Category Percentile
Underweight < 5%
Normal weight 5% - 85%
At risk of overweight 85% - 95%
Overweight > 95%

To find the BMI, start by selecting the right chart below depending on whether it is for a boy or a girl. Find the age in the bottom row. Climb upwards until you meet the desired percentile curve. BMI is the value written horizontally on the sides of the chart. 

Example: What's the upper limit of a normal BMI for an 11 year old boy? 

1. Find the percentile. This is the upper limit of normal. So 85%.
2. Locate the chart for boys below.
3. Locate the row for 11-year-old boys in the graph.
4. Go up until crossing the 85th percentile curve.
5. Read the corresponding value horizontally on the sides. We read a value of about 20.1 kg/m2

So a BMI of 20.1 kg/m2 would be considered the upper normal limit for an 11 year old boy.

How to convert BMI to body fat percentage(BFP)?

Although the BMI is only an approximation, it is possible to give an idea of ​​the percentage of body fat with the following formulas:

For boys:
BFP = 1.51 × BMI - 0.70 × Age - 2.2

For girls:
BFP = 1.51 × BMI - 0.70 × Age + 1.4

For adult men:
BFP = 1.20 × BMI + 0.23 × Age - 16.2

For adult women:
BFP = 1.20 × BMI + 0.23 × Age - 5.4

What's underweight?

Underweight is defined by the World Health Organization as the abnormal condition of individuals with a body mass index between 15 and 18.5.
Being underweight increases the risk of diseases including fatigue, hair loss, reduced immune function, osteoporosis and an increased risk of surgical complications.
The most common causes of underweight are malnutrition, drug or alcohol addiction, smoking and poverty, and mental health or personal hygiene problems.
In the fashion industry in Israel all models with a BMI below 18.5 must obtain a medical certificate to determine that they are healthy enough to work. Other countries like France, Spain, Italy and Denmark have passed similar laws. 

What's overweight and obesity?

Overweight is due to too much adipose (fatty) mass. Obesity is an even more excessive accumulation of fat.
Being overweight can increase the risk of cardiovascular disease. Obese people are at an increased risk of developing many diseases and health problems, including cardiovascular disease, high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, gallstones, breathing problems, and some cancers. 

What's the link between weight and physical and psychological well-being?

Focusing on lifestyle changes like eating a healthy diet, monitoring portion sizes, being physically active, and reducing sedentary time is effective in lowering your risk of developing health problems.

This healthy lifestyle helps you feel good about yourself and gives you more energy to enjoy life. 
Physical activity helps increase brain function and improve emotional well-being.

What can I do if I'm worried about my BMI result?

You may need to pay more attention to your diet and / or the amount of physical activity.
If you are overweight or obese, know that even a small loss of weight can reduce your risk of contracting several diseases.
If you are underweight, gaining weight could be beneficial for your health.
If you are worried about your BMI or find that it is not classifying you properly, talk to a healthcare professional. 

What are the limitations of BMI?

There's no standard ideal healthy weight for everyone. Each person is different. 
For most people, BMI works well. However, it has a tendency to ...

overestimate body fat:

  • In men. 
  • Among African Americans. 
  • In athletes and others who have a muscular build.

And underestimate body fat:

  • In women.
  • Among Asians. 
  • In seniors and others who have lost muscle mass.

The use of BMI is not recommended in the following cases:

  • During pregnancy and lactation. Due to the change in body composition.
  • Those at the ends of the size and weight spectrum. 
  • People aged 65 and over.

How to measure body fat more accurately?

There are many techniques for measuring body fat, ranging from the simplest but less precise method (US navy method) to the more complicated and precise (computed tomography). 

You can purchase the necessary equipment to perform these measurements at home (scale with body composition analysis or body fat caliper). But it might not be necessary: ​​many gyms and doctor's offices are equipped with devices to measure the percentage of body fat. 

US Navy method

This method was developed at the Naval Health Research Center by Hodgdon and Beckett in 1984. It combines measurements of body height, neck circumference, waist and hips to estimate the percentage of body fat using the following formulas: 

Body Fat Percentage Formula (BFP) For Men: 

US System: 

BFP = 86.010 X log10(abdomen - neck) - 70.041 X log10(height) + 36.76 

Metric system: 

BFP = 495 1.0324 - 0.19077×log10(waist-neck) ) + 0.15456 × log10(height) - 450

Body Fat Percentage Formula (BFP) For Women: 

US System: 

BFP = 163.205×log10(waist+hip-neck) - 97.684×(log10(height)) - 78.387

Metric system: 

BFP = 495 1.29579 - 0.35004×log10(waist+hip-neck) + 0.22100 × log10(height) - 450

The US navy body fat test is 1-3% accurate for most people.

Body Fat Scales
Scales with body composition analysis use a technology called bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA). While stepping on the scale, a weak electric current runs through the legs. Fat conducts much less electricity than water and muscle. So when the scale measures more resistance, it detects a higher level of body fat.
Combined with height, weight, age and gender, the scale then uses an equation to provide the percentage of body fat.
The results of these scales vary greatly compared to the results of other methods of measuring body fat percentage.
Body fat caliper
A skin fold test is done using a tool called a caliper to pinch different areas of the body. Calipers measure the thickness of subcutaneous fat (the fat under the skin) in certain areas of the body. Measurements are taken at 3 or 7 different places on the body. A formula then gives the corresponding body fat rate.
When the test is performed correctly, the error rate is approximately 5 percent.

Hydrodensitometry
Using Archimedes principle, the subject is immersed entirely, in a brief manner, in a tank of water and the body volume is determined from the volume of water displaced. Knowing the subject's weight and volume, therefore its density, the body fat rate can be calculated.
Hydrodensitometry is very accurate: less than 1% error for adults and children.

Dual-energy X-ray absorbiometry (DXA)
DXA measures the transmission through the body of low and high energy X-rays. Bones and soft tissues are affected differently depending on the two kinds of rays. It then becomes possible to deduce the percentage of fatty and non-fatty masses according to the two scans.
The level of precision is comparable to that of hydrodensitometry.

Air displacement plethysmography
This method uses the displacement of air rather than immersion in water.
The device includes an egg-shaped chamber that completely surrounds the body. Once the body density is determined by weight and volume, the machine uses this data to calculate the percentage of body fat.
Air displacement plethysmography is as accurate as hydrodensitometry.

MRI or CT scan
These machines take cross-sectional images of the body and can even measure intra-abdominal fat.
These tests are very expensive but are currently the most accurate method to determine the percentage of body fat.

Disclosure

BMI can be a useful screening tool, but it cannot diagnose an individual's body fat level or health. To determine if BMI is a risk to your health, a healthcare professional should perform additional assessments. These assessments include measurements of skinfold thickness, assessments of diet, physical activity, and family history.
